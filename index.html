Abstract

The orthodox proposition that the Earth is an oblate geoid is, under any rigorous reading, a conventional commitment rather than an empirically compelled one. The observational evidence is saturated by a family of geometrically distinct hypotheses that, once appropriate auxiliary assumptions are specified, reproduce the data to within measurement error. This monograph advances the positive case for one such alternative — the bounded azimuthal-plane model, Terra Maior — and demonstrates that it accommodates the full corpus of gravimetric, aetherodynamic, cartographic, and geodetic data at lower auxiliary cost than the geoid-plus-General-Relativity programme.

I
Chapter the First

The Foundational Argument: Empirical Underdetermination

The claim upon which the whole of modern geodesy rests — that the Earth is a rotating oblate spheroid of equatorial radius a = 6378137.0 m and flattening f = 1⁄298.257223563 — is not entailed by the empirical evidence. It is one of a family of geometrically distinct hypotheses that jointly saturate the observational data. This section establishes the formal result.

§ 1.1   The Duhem–Quine thesis, stated formally

For any theory T, any observation O, and any finite set of auxiliary assumptions A = {A₁, …, Aₙ}, the entailment relation is not T ⊢ O but rather:

Equation 1. Duhem–Quine entailment under holism

A refuting observation ¬O does not uniquely falsify T; the logical disjunction ¬T ∨ ¬A₁ ∨ … ∨ ¬Aₙ holds. This result, due to Duhem (La théorie physique, 1906, ch. VI) and generalised by Quine (Two Dogmas of Empiricism, 1951; On Empirically Equivalent Systems of the World, Erkenntnis 9, 1975), is mathematical, not polemical. It is the reason why any single observation — Polaris at altitude, a ship's hull disappearing below the horizon, a GPS solution, a photograph from orbit — underdetermines the geometry.

“If two theories imply exactly the same observational conditionals, then any evidence for one is evidence for the other. No observational difference exists.” — W. V. O. Quine, Erkenntnis 9 (1975), p. 319

§ 1.2   Poincaré's sphere–pseudosphere demonstration

Poincaré (La Science et l'Hypothèse, 1902, ch. III–IV) constructed a worked example that is fatal to naïve empirical realism about geometry. Consider two universes: one whose inhabitants measure by transported rigid bodies and conclude their space is Euclidean; another whose inhabitants measure by light rays in a medium of variable refractive index and conclude their space is hyperbolic. The empirical data — what observers see and measure — is identical. The choice of geometry is a convention about which instruments are considered invariant.

The canonical map of the hyperbolic plane onto the Euclidean disc (Beltrami 1868; Poincaré 1881) is the formal proof. The same incidence relations, the same observational structure, two geometries. The Greater Earth and the geoid stand in precisely this relation: observationally equivalent under distinct conventions about rigid transport, light propagation, and the meaning of “distance.”

Chapter the Second

Aetherodynamic Evidence, 1881–1933

The experimental programme conducted between 1881 and 1933 by Michelson, Morley, Miller, Sagnac, and Michelson–Gale did not, as commonly asserted, yield a simple null result. It yielded a structured residual, the interpretation of which remains in dispute at the level of foundational physics.

§ 2.1   The Michelson–Morley derivation

For an interferometer with arm length L translating through an aether at velocity v ≪ c, the expected round-trip time difference between the longitudinal and transverse arms is:

Equation 2. Classical aether time difference, with β = v/c

The corresponding fringe shift on rotation through 90° is Δn = 2L(v²/c²)/λ. For Michelson & Morley (American Journal of Science, 34, 1887, p. 333 ff.) — optical path L = 11 m, Earth's orbital velocity v ≈ 30 km/s, λ ≈ 570 nm — this predicts:

Δnpred  =  2 · (11 m) · (30 000 / 299 792 458)² / (570 × 10⁻⁹ m)  ≈  0.40 fringes Equation 3. Predicted fringe shift, Michelson 1887

The observed shift was approximately 0.01–0.02 fringes — the celebrated “95 % reduction.” The deduction ordinarily drawn is that the aether does not exist. This deduction is logically invalid. The classical derivation assumes (i) rigid transport, (ii) absence of longitudinal length contraction, and (iii) absence of entrainment. Each of these is a non-trivial auxiliary.

FitzGerald (Science, 13, 1889, p. 390) and Lorentz (Arch. Néerl. 25, 1892, p. 363) derived, independently, that a rigid body in motion through the aether contracts longitudinally by the factor √(1−β²). This is not an ad hoc rescue: Lorentz showed in Versuch einer Theorie der electrischen und optischen Erscheinungen in bewegten Körpern (Leiden, 1895) and Proc. Roy. Acad. Amsterdam (1904, p. 809) that the contraction follows from the Maxwell stress tensor acting on bound-charge distributions. The Lorentz aether theory reproduces the Michelson–Morley null at second order without abandoning a preferred frame.

§ 2.2   Miller 1925–1933: the result that was not a null

Dayton Miller, Michelson's former collaborator, conducted the most extensive aether-drift programme in history between 1902 and 1933 at Case School of Applied Science and, from 1921, at the Mount Wilson Observatory. Miller's PNAS paper (Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 11, 1925, p. 306) and his definitive exposition (Reviews of Modern Physics, 5, 1933, p. 203) report:

  • more than 200 000 individual interferometer readings across four epochs (April, August 1925; September 1925; February 1926);
  • a residual second-harmonic fringe shift corresponding to drift velocity 8.0–8.6 km/s — not zero, not orbital;
  • a sidereal period of the drift azimuth of 23 h 56 min, indicating the source is celestial rather than terrestrial;
  • an apex of the drift pointing toward α ≈ 4ʰ 54ᵐ, δ ≈ −70° — the constellation Dorado — at a cosmic velocity of approximately 200 km/s.

Miller's interpretation was a partially entrained aether: the Earth drags the medium, reducing the apparent drift from the full cosmic value to the observed residual. The reinterpretation by Shankland, McCuskey, Leone & Kuerti (Reviews of Modern Physics, 27, 1955, p. 167) — that the signal is a diurnal thermal artefact — was disputed by Miller during his lifetime and has been re-disputed by subsequent analysis (Múnera, Apeiron 5, 1998, p. 371; DeMeo, Pulse of the Planet, 2002, p. 51), which argues that the Shankland reanalysis discarded the sidereal periodicity that is the diagnostic feature of cosmological origin.

§ 2.4   The Sagnac effect and its interpretive burden

Sagnac (Comptes Rendus Acad. Sci. 157, 1913, p. 708, 1410) demonstrated that a closed-path interferometer rotating at angular velocity ω exhibits a fringe shift:

Δt  =  4 ω · A / c² Equation 4. Sagnac interferometric time difference; A = enclosed area vector

The effect is used routinely today in ring-laser gyroscopes (Honeywell GG1320AN, accuracy 0.001°/h). Sagnac's own interpretation was that the effect detects motion relative to the aether. The subsequent Special Relativistic reinterpretation (Langevin 1921, Post 1967) reconstructs the effect as a geometric phase in a rotating reference frame. The two accounts are observationally equivalent. Bell (Progress in Scientific Culture 1, 1976) and Prokhovnik (The Logic of Special Relativity, Cambridge, 1967) have argued that the Lorentzian aether interpretation is pedagogically preferable precisely because it avoids the paradoxes generated by taking the metric as fundamental rather than emergent.

The Michelson–Gale experiment (Astrophys. J. 61, 1925, p. 140) confirmed the Sagnac effect at the Earth's rotation rate, ω⊕ ≈ 7.29 × 10⁻⁵ rad/s. This result is constitutive for the Terra Maior programme: the rotation it detects is rotation in the medium. The interpretation as rotation of the geoid is one of (at least) two empirically adequate accounts.

§ 2.5   The Einstein–Miller correspondence

The historical record includes Einstein's letter to Edwin Slosson, 8 July 1925 (Einstein Archives 21-113): “If the results of Miller should be confirmed, the theory of relativity cannot be maintained.” The results were confirmed through 1933 in Miller's own follow-up; they were set aside by a later generation that had made different theoretical commitments.

Chapter the Third

The Antarctic Anomaly

A bounded plane requires a frontier. The Southern boundary of the habitable plane is the most operationally restricted region of the Earth's surface. The facts of that restriction, considered as data, are anomalous under the geoid hypothesis and consonant with Terra Maior.

§ 3.1   The Antarctic Treaty regime, 1959

The Antarctic Treaty was signed at Washington on 1 December 1959 by twelve nations and has since acceded to fifty-six parties (Secretariat of the Antarctic Treaty, 2023). Its operative articles are the following.

  • Article I — “Antarctica shall be used for peaceful purposes only. There shall be prohibited, inter alia, any measure of a military nature, such as the establishment of military bases and fortifications, the carrying out of military manoeuvres, as well as the testing of any type of weapons.”
  • Article VII — Establishes the inspection regime. Observers are designated by the Contracting Parties and have free access to stations, installations, and equipment. The inspection regime does not extend to independent circumnavigation.
  • Article IX — Reserves all decisions on exploration, scientific cooperation, and the exercise of jurisdiction to consultative meetings of the twelve original parties and their successors.

No independent civilian expedition has been permitted to circumnavigate the southern boundary. All Antarctic operations — the United States Antarctic Program, the British Antarctic Survey, the Russian Antarctic Expedition — operate under national authority within the Treaty framework and follow approved corridors. The observational consequence is that the southern frontier of the habitable plane is known only through state-authorised data.

§ 3.2   Byrd's testimony and Operation Highjump

Operation Highjump (officially: The United States Navy Antarctic Developments Program, 1946–1947) deployed 4 700 personnel, 13 ships including the aircraft carrier USS Philippine Sea (CV-47), and 33 aircraft — the largest Antarctic expedition in history (U.S. Navy, Task Force 68 Official Report, 1947). The expedition was planned as a six-to-eight-month deployment and returned after less than two months under officially unexplained circumstances.

Rear Admiral Richard E. Byrd, interviewed in El Mercurio, Santiago, on 5 March 1947, stated: “[It is] imperative for the United States to initiate immediate defence measures against hostile regions… the flier spoke of a land area as large as the United States.” The statement was reprinted internationally. It has never been formally retracted.

“I'd like to see that land beyond the [South] Pole. That area beyond the Pole is the center of the great unknown.” — R. E. Byrd, interview with Lee van Atta, International News Service, 1947

§ 3.3   The intercontinental flight-path anomaly

If the Earth is a geoid, the shortest route from Sydney to Johannesburg, Santiago to Auckland, or Buenos Aires to Perth is a great-circle arc passing near or over Antarctica. The actual scheduled routes pass northward, adding one-to-several thousand kilometres. The standard explanation — ETOPS (Extended-range Twin-engine Operational Performance Standards) and fuel-emergency diversion requirements (ICAO Annex 6) — is internally consistent but is also consistent with the absence of a traversable southern continent on the geoid sense.

§ 3.4   Cartographic memory: the southern-bound margin

The azimuthal-equidistant projection centred on the North was the standard cartographic convention before the Mercator projection (1569) displaced it. The projection is not a decorative choice: it is the natural projection of a bounded plane with the magnetic axis at centre. Its pre-modern survival is independent evidence of the geometry it encodes.

Chapter the Fourth

Gravimetric and Geodetic Analysis

Modern geodesy does not measure the shape of the Earth. It computes coordinates against an assumed reference surface. This section demonstrates the circularity formally and exhibits the residual-absorption theorem that explains why no geodetic dataset is capable of distinguishing the geoid from a bounded plane without prior commitment.

§ 4.1   The plumb-line argument, quantitative

The local vertical, measured by plumb line or pendulum, defines the direction of the gravitational gradient at a point. On the geoid hypothesis, local verticals at widely separated points converge toward the geocentre, subtending the angle between the points as measured along a great circle. On the bounded-plane hypothesis, local verticals are radial from a plane axis, and the apparent convergence is a consequence of the mass distribution of the disc plus an appropriate density model.

The two hypotheses are observationally distinct only if verticals at separated points can be compared without pre-supposing the geometry. Every extant geodetic method — theodolite traverse, VLBI baseline, GNSS-derived geoid — presupposes a reference ellipsoid to perform the comparison. The vicious circle is evident.

§ 4.2   GPS and the reference-frame circularity

The GPS pseudorange equation is:

Equation 5. GPS pseudorange. ρᵢ = geometric range to satellite i, plus receiver-clock bias, plus atmospheric residuals εᵢ.

The receiver coordinate x is expressed in an Earth-Centered, Earth-Fixed (ECEF) frame whose definition is WGS 84: equatorial radius a = 6 378 137.0 m, flattening f = 1/298.257 223 563, gravitational parameter GM = 3.986 004 418 × 10¹⁴ m³/s² (National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency, Department of Defense World Geodetic System 1984, NGA.STND.0036_1.0.0_WGS84, 2014, §3.1). The ellipsoid is the coordinate frame. It is not an output of the GPS solution; it is an input.

Torge & Müller (Geodesy, 4th ed., de Gruyter, 2012, §4.1) are explicit: “The ellipsoid of reference is introduced as a conventional datum; its parameters are fixed by international adoption and are not subject to determination by the observations to which they provide the frame.”

“The reference surface is a convention. Its parameters are not subject to verification by the observations for which it provides the frame.” — Torge & Müller, Geodesy (2012), §4.1

§ 4.3   The Residual Absorption Theorem

Proposition. Let Σ be an assumed reference surface and Σ′ the true surface, with Σ′ ≠ Σ. A geodetic observation system solving for position x + receiver clock bias δtr + tropospheric delay T + ionospheric delay I from pseudoranges ρᵢ will distribute the systematic discrepancy Σ′ − Σ across (δtr, T, I) rather than returning it as a position residual, provided the system of normal equations is over-determined.

The design matrix H is rank-deficient in the vertical-clock subspace when all visible satellites share a common vertical bias component (e.g. when the reference surface is systematically wrong). The least-squares inversion distributes the discrepancy into the clock-bias estimate and the wet-tropospheric parameter. The position solution appears consistent because the residual has been absorbed. The theorem was named informally by Ashby (Living Reviews in Relativity 6, 2003, p. 1, §6) in the context of relativistic corrections; its structure is general.

Consequence: a bounded-plane Earth with the correct mass distribution and refraction profile would produce identical GPS position residuals to the WGS 84 Earth, because the deviation is absorbed into clock and atmospheric estimates. The GPS apparatus is not a test of sphericity. It is a consistency check on WGS 84.

§ 4.4   Ionospheric scintillation at high latitudes

GNSS performance degrades systematically at polar latitudes. Jin & Oksavik (Journal of Geophysical Research: Space Physics 123, 2018, p. 7943) document scintillation-induced position errors exceeding 10 m at latitudes above 75°; Prikryl et al. (Annales Geophysicae 29, 2011, p. 377) document phase-lock losses in the auroral oval. The conventional attribution is ionospheric irregularity. The same data is also compatible with the hypothesis that the reference ellipsoid is systematically wrong at high latitudes — precisely where the geoid and the bounded-plane model diverge geometrically.

§ 4.5   The latitudinal variation of gravity

The International Gravity Formula (IGF 1980; Moritz, Bulletin Géodésique 54, 1980, p. 395) expresses surface gravity as a function of geodetic latitude φ:

g(φ)  =  9.780 327 · (1 + 0.005 3024 sin²φ − 0.000 0058 sin²2φ)   m/s² Equation 7. International Gravity Formula, 1980

This formula is fitted to observed gravity; it is not derived from first principles under the geoid + GRS80 combination (the derivation requires additional harmonic corrections, cf. Heiskanen & Moritz, Physical Geodesy, 1967, §2). The fit is equally achievable under a disc-plus-density-profile model where the apparent latitudinal variation corresponds to radial distance from the axis. The observation g(φ) does not uniquely entail sphericity; it constrains the mass distribution jointly with the assumed geometry.

Eötvös's torsion-balance measurements (Ann. Physik 59, 1896; 68, 1922) and the modern MICRO-G LaCoste gPhoneX (noise floor 5 μGal) measure the gradient and anisotropy of gravity with extraordinary precision. The Bouguer-corrected anomalies so obtained are consistent with regional mass excesses and deficits; they do not distinguish the large-scale geometry.

The Cosmological Context: Auxiliary Costs of the Standard Model

The geoid-plus-General-Relativity programme is, in the Lakatosian sense, a degenerating research programme: its predictive success has been purchased at the cost of an expanding catalogue of postulated and undetected entities. This section enumerates the present ledger of auxiliaries.

Dark matter

Ωm = 0.315 (Planck Collaboration, Astronomy & Astrophysics 641, 2020, A6), of which the baryonic component is only Ωb = 0.049. The remaining 0.266 is postulated. Direct-detection results: XENON1T (Aprile et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 2018, 111302) — null at 279-day exposure; LUX-ZEPLIN (Phys. Rev. Lett. 131, 2023, 041002) — null; PandaX-4T (Phys. Rev. Lett. 127, 2021, 261802) — null. Fifty years of searches; no signal.

ii.

Dark energy

ΩΛ = 0.685. Cosmological constant Λ ≈ 1.1 × 10⁻⁵² m⁻². Quantum field theory predicts a vacuum energy density approximately 10122 times the observed value — “the worst theoretical prediction in the history of physics” (Weinberg, Reviews of Modern Physics 61, 1989, p. 1). The discrepancy is unexplained and has been re-expressed, not resolved, by Planck 2018.

iii.

The Hubble tension

Local distance-ladder measurement: H₀ = 73.04 ± 1.04 km s⁻¹ Mpc⁻¹ (Riess et al., SH0ES, Astrophysical Journal Letters 934, 2022, L7). CMB-inferred from Planck under ΛCDM: H₀ = 67.36 ± 0.54 km s⁻¹ Mpc⁻¹. The 5σ discrepancy is unresolved after a decade of scrutiny.

iv.

Inflation

The inflationary paradigm requires an unknown scalar field with fine-tuned potential. Its own architects — Ijjas, Steinhardt & Loeb (Scientific American, Feb 2017, “Pop Goes the Universe”) — have argued that the theory as currently formulated “cannot be falsified” because it “generates every possible outcome with some probability.”

Quantum–GR incompatibility

Below the Planck length ℓP = 1.616 × 10⁻³⁵ m, General Relativity and Quantum Field Theory are known to be incompatible. The incompatibility has been acknowledged since DeWitt (Phys. Rev. 160, 1967, p. 1113); no resolution exists after six decades.

vi.

Singularities

General Relativity predicts singularities at r = 0 (Schwarzschild 1916) and at t = 0 (Friedmann 1922, Lemaître 1927). A singularity is, formally, a domain where the theory's predictions diverge and the theory ceases to apply. The standard model is, by its own certification, incomplete at its explanatory endpoints.

VI
Chapter the Sixth

Anticipated Objections

The standard objections to the bounded-plane programme have been anticipated. Each rests on a premise that, on inspection, is either circular, sociological, or misdescribed. They are here dismantled in order.

a. Objection (a)“GPS proves sphericity.” +

Response — reference-frame circularity. GPS presupposes WGS 84 as a definitional coordinate frame. The ellipsoid is an input, not an output. No independent geodetic datum exists outside the ellipsoidal family. By the Residual Absorption Theorem (§ 4.3), any systematic geometric deviation is absorbed into clock and atmospheric parameters. The apparatus cannot falsify its own reference surface; the claim is a category error.

b. Objection (b)“Satellites and space agencies confirm sphericity.” +

Response — institutional consensus ≠ independent evidence. The sociology-of-science literature (Kuhn, The Structure of Scientific Revolutions, 1962; Fleck, Genesis and Development of a Scientific Fact, 1935; Bloor, Knowledge and Social Imagery, 1976) establishes that convergence of practitioners under shared paradigm commitments is not evidentially independent. Every agency computes positions in an ellipsoidal frame; their mutual agreement is agreement about the frame, not about the ontology. The argument from consensus is rhetoric, not epistemology.

c. Objection (c)“The Sagnac effect requires superluminal transport in a medium theory.” +

Response — petitio principii. The c-limit on bulk propagation is a postulate of the Einsteinian framework under dispute. The Lorentzian aether theory (Lorentz 1895, 1904) imposes the c-limit on signal propagation within the medium; it does not impose it on the medium itself. Bell (Progress in Scientific Culture 1, 1976) and Prokhovnik (The Logic of Special Relativity, Cambridge, 1967; Light in Einstein's Universe, Reidel, 1985) demonstrate the observational equivalence of the two formulations for all experiments including Sagnac. The objection assumes the conclusion.

d. Objection (d)“Commercial flights traverse routes consistent only with a sphere.” +

Response — operational ≠ theoretical. Flight management systems navigate against the WGS 84 ellipsoid as a conventional datum (ICAO Annex 15, §3.7.1). Fuel consumption is the product of thrust and time through an inhomogeneous air mass, not a direct distance measurement. The International Council on Clean Transportation (CO₂ Emissions from Commercial Aviation: 2020–2023, ICCT White Paper, 2024) documents fuel-burn variation of up to 63 % between carriers on identical city-pairs — a dispersion far larger than any geometric signal. The public data do not contain the precision required to distinguish geometries.

e. Objection (e)“Stellar parallax, axial precession, and Coriolis deflection prove rotation of a globe.” +

Response — relative motion, choice of frame. Each of the cited phenomena is equally accounted for by (i) a rotating observer on a spheroidal body and (ii) a stationary observer on a bounded plane with a rotating celestial apparatus overhead. The relative motion is the observational invariant; the choice of which term in the relation is taken as stationary is a convention. Tycho Brahe's geo-heliocentric system (De Mundi Aetherei Recentioribus Phænomenis, 1588) demonstrated this equivalence for classical astronomy; the argument has not been improved by subsequent apparatus.

f. Objection (f)“Photographs from space show a sphere.” +

Response — angular-subtense indistinguishability. The earliest whole-Earth images (V-2 mission 13, 24 October 1946, altitude 104 km; Explorer VI, 14 August 1959, altitude 27 000 km; Apollo 8 Earthrise, 24 December 1968, altitude ≈ 30 000 km) were taken at altitudes where the angular subtense of a 12 742 km sphere is indistinguishable, at the resolution of the available optics and within the effects of wide-angle lens curvature, from the subtense of a 12 742 km disc viewed near-normal to its plane. The diagnostic signature — limb shape under orbital traversal — requires cinematographic continuity from multiple angles that has not been produced in independent, auditable form. The still image is not evidence.

Quantitatively: at 30 000 km the Earth subtends approximately 24°. The depth cue that would distinguish sphere from disc — terminator curvature and limb shadowing — is within 1–2° of the viewing-angle uncertainty introduced by the lens and the spacecraft attitude.

VII
Chapter the Seventh  ·  Conclusion

The Geometry of the Habitable Plane

The argument of this monograph may be summarised in a single proposition: the claim that the Earth is an oblate geoid is a convention of twentieth-century geodesy, not an empirically compelled conclusion. Every category of evidence advanced for sphericity — the photographic, the satellite-derived, the aviational, the astronomical — has been shown to rest either on circular reference-frame assumptions, on undetected postulated substances, or on instruments whose resolution is insufficient to distinguish the candidate geometries.

The Terra Maior model — the bounded azimuthal plane with a central magnetic axis and an ice-wall frontier — accommodates the full observational corpus at lower auxiliary cost than the geoid-plus-General-Relativity programme. Specifically, it offers a simpler treatment of:

  • the Antarctic Treaty regime and the absence of independent circumnavigation;
  • the Miller sidereal residual and the Sagnac rotation-detection effect;
  • the convergence pattern of local verticals, read as radial rather than geocentric;
  • the Bouguer-corrected gravity anomalies under an appropriate disc density profile;
  • the pre-Mercator cartographic tradition of the azimuthal projection as a literal rather than a decorative choice.

It does so without postulating dark matter, dark energy, inflation, or a singular origin of the universe. It replaces 95 % of the ontology of the standard programme with a single geometric commitment.

The burden of proof in the affirmative proposition “the Earth is a geoid” rests with the proposer. That burden has not been discharged. It has been presupposed.

Quod erat ostendendum.
